Section 1:
16 Dozen Cans of Soda on the Wall

This album represents the culmination of about 5 months of work by a half dozen people. So it took three cameras to properly document the event, so we'd like to thank everybody who let us borrow their pictures. Now, for some backstory:

Jeremy and Greg were drinking a lot of soda. So we all decided to put the accumulated cans to use. We needed a target: Brad.

     
Initially, the cans were slow to accumulate. However, we bought some hot glue and began to assemble them covertly in Jeremy's room.
Here, Greg sketches out the final plan: a door baricade to fit snugly in Brad's doorframe while he's not looking.
Oh, the humanity. Or, rather, calories. Check out this montage of pictures, showing about 1/3 of the progress we made.
Look at that self-supporting wall. VERY NICE!
Greg gets in on some of that hot hot glue action.
Then, 5 months after the cans began to accumulate, it was go time.
Fortunately, it had been assembled in 4 easy to transport sections for a wonderfully modular can/wall experience.
People looked on in awe as we passed with our prefabricated can sections. Mitch was especially taken aback.
Waiting for the elevator.
Riding upwards... in style.
Upon our arrival on the third floor, we proceeded to assemble the parts into two sections for quick and stealthy final positioning.
We needed to make sure everything was straight and level.
A test fit couldn't hurt.
And then we were ready to move in for the kill. Here, Peter queues up in the hall. If you look carefully, Jeremy is fixing Peter's camera in the only way that really works: smacking it a few times.

CEE 2814 - Measurements
One of the classes I'm taking this year is CEE Measurements. It's essentially a surveying class.
One of the labs that we have to do involves making a topographic map. It took two lab periods, and about 12 hours of meetings over the weekend before it was due to put it together.
We took almost 200 points of data with our really expensive (don't break it!) surveying equipment.
First, we plotted the points on a 2x3 foot piece of grid paper. Then we connected the dots and figured out where along those lines the two foot contour lines went, based on some linear interpolation. It was really nifty to see how it worked out.
We then connected the contour lines and the dots indicating the planimetric features (parking lots and whatnot).
We then smoothed out the contour lines using a set of French curves. It looked really cool, and here's a larger version.
